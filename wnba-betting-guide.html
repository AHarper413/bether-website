Why WNBA Betting is Perfect for Women

WNBA Season Structure & Betting Calendar

Understanding the WNBA season helps you identify the best betting opportunities throughout the year:

Regular Season (May - September)

  • 40 games per team: Shorter season means every game matters more
  • Commissioner's Cup: Mid-season tournament with extra betting markets
  • All-Star Break: Great time for futures betting and season awards
  • Olympic Break: Schedule disruption can create betting value

Playoffs (September - October)

  • Best-of-3 first round: Lower seeds can cause upsets
  • Best-of-5 semifinals: More games mean more betting opportunities
  • Best-of-5 WNBA Finals: Peak betting interest and coverage

Types of WNBA Bets Explained

Here are the most popular WNBA betting options, explained specifically for women's basketball:

Moneyline Bets

Simply pick which team will win the game. Perfect for beginners.

Example:

Las Vegas Aces -150 vs. Connecticut Sun +130

Bet $150 on Aces to win $100, or bet $100 on Sun to win $130

Point Spread

Bet on whether a team will win or lose by more than a set number of points.

Example:

New York Liberty -3.5 vs. Indiana Fever +3.5

Liberty must win by 4+ points, or Fever must lose by 3 or fewer (or win)

Over/Under (Totals)

Bet on whether the combined score will be over or under a set number.

Example:

Seattle Storm vs. Phoenix Mercury O/U 165.5

If total score is 166+, Over wins. If 165 or less, Under wins

Player Props

Bet on individual player performances - points, rebounds, assists.

Example:

Caitlin Clark Over 22.5 Points

If she scores 23+, you win. If 22 or fewer, you lose

Championship Futures

Bet on which team will win the WNBA championship before/during the season.

Example:

New York Liberty +300 to win WNBA Championship

Bet $100 to win $300 if Liberty wins the title

Season Awards

Bet on individual awards like MVP, Rookie of the Year, Coach of the Year.

Example:

Caitlin Clark +200 for Rookie of the Year

Bet $100 to win $200 if Clark wins ROY
