NFL Betting Guide

Master football betting with strategies designed for America's most popular sport. From point spreads to player props, learn how to bet on the NFL with confidence.

Why NFL Betting is Perfect for Beginners

The NFL offers the most betting opportunities of any sport, with games primarily on Sundays giving you time to research. The league's popularity means extensive coverage and statistics are available, making it easier to make informed decisions.

NFL Season Structure

18 weeks of regular season (September-January) + playoffs. Each team plays 17 games, giving you plenty of opportunities to learn and apply strategies throughout the season.

Understanding NFL Point Spreads

Point spreads are the most popular NFL bet type. The favorite must win by more than the spread, while the underdog can lose by less than the spread or win outright.

NFL Spread Example

Kansas City Chiefs -7.5 (-110)

Denver Broncos +7.5 (-110)

Chiefs must win by 8+ points to cover. Broncos cover if they lose by 7 or fewer, or win the game.

Key Spread Numbers in NFL

Certain numbers are more common in NFL final scores:

Key Number Why It Matters
3 Field goal (most common margin)
7 Touchdown + extra point
10 Touchdown + field goal
14 Two touchdowns

NFL Totals (Over/Under)

Betting on whether the combined score will be over or under the sportsbook's number. This bet type is great for beginners because you're not picking sides.

Factors That Affect Totals

Weather: Wind, rain, and snow typically lower scoring

Pace of Play: Fast teams run more plays, creating more scoring opportunities

Defensive Strength: Check rankings for points allowed per game

Injuries: Key offensive players missing can significantly impact scoring

Total Example

Bills vs Dolphins - Total 47.5

Over 47.5: Combined score 48 or higher

Under 47.5: Combined score 47 or lower

Popular NFL Player Props

Player props let you bet on individual performances rather than game outcomes. These can be easier to predict than full game results.

Passing Yards

Will a QB throw for over/under a set number of yards? Consider matchup and weather.

Rushing Yards

Running back yardage totals. Check opponent's run defense ranking and injury reports.

Receiving Yards

Wide receiver/tight end totals. Look at target share and matchup data.

Touchdown Scorers

Anytime or first touchdown scorer bets. Red zone usage is key data point.

NFL Research Strategy

Successful NFL betting requires consistent research. Here's what to focus on:

Weekly Preparation

Tuesday-Wednesday: Review injury reports and news

Thursday-Friday: Check weather forecasts for outdoor games

Saturday-Sunday: Look for line movements and public betting trends

Key Statistics to Track

Points scored/allowed per game | Yards per play (offense and defense) | Turnover differential | Red zone efficiency | Third down conversion rates

Situational Factors

Divisional Games: Often closer than expected, familiarity breeds parity

Prime Time: Teams sometimes perform differently under lights

Travel: West Coast teams traveling east for early games can struggle

Bye Weeks: Teams coming off bye weeks are typically well-prepared

NFL Betting Calendar

Understanding the NFL season helps you plan your betting approach:

Preseason (August)

Avoid betting on preseason games - they're unpredictable with mixed rosters and varying effort levels.

Early Season (Weeks 1-4)

Small sample sizes make teams hard to evaluate. Focus on fundamental matchups and coaching advantages.

Mid-Season (Weeks 5-12)

Best time for data-driven betting. You have enough stats to make informed decisions.

Late Season (Weeks 13-18)

Playoff implications matter. Some teams may rest players in Week 18 if playoff position is set.

Playoffs

Higher stakes mean more motivated play, but also increased public betting that can move lines.

Managing Your NFL Bankroll

The NFL season is a marathon, not a sprint. Proper bankroll management is crucial for long-term success.

Weekly Betting Strategy

With 16-17 games most Sundays, it's tempting to bet every game. Instead, focus on 2-3 games you've researched thoroughly. Quality over quantity always wins.

Consider the long season when setting bet sizes. A 5% rule (5% of bankroll per bet) gives you staying power through inevitable losing streaks.
